Maori Learner Success - Cultural Capability Resources

Supporting learners, educational outcomes, Maori learner success, adult and community education, literacy and numeracy skills, teacher professional development, matauranga Maori, Maori pedagogies, tikanga Maori, te reo Maori, kaupapa Maori, values-based learning.

--- PROJECT DETAILS ---
Information and resources related to cultural capability in adult literacy and numeracy education.

Covers topics such as using Maori pedagogies and culturally responsive teaching and learning methods, including the model of matauranga Maori by Professor Mason Durie known as Te Whare Tapa Wha.

--- KEY POINTS TO NOTE ---
- Demonstrating cultural capability in teaching programmes enhances learner engagement and promotes learner success.
- These resources aim to support the development of cultural capability.
- To get started, watch the Introduction to Engaging Maori Learners video.
- There are several resources available to support cultural capability, including videos and written resources to download.

--- RESOURCES LISTED ON THE PAGE ---

   A YouTube video explaining how to engage Maori learners.

   A YouTube video explaining the powhiri process. It links this process to the experiences of Maori learners, and others, as they approach tertiary education.

   A YouTube video explaining how the wharenui can be used to teach literacy.

   A YouTube video explaining the concept of Tuakana-Teina.

   A YouTube video explaining the concept of poutama.

6. Maori Learner Success, Te Whare Tapa Wha
   A YouTube video explaining Professor Mason Durie's model of matauranga Maori.

7. Maori Cultural Capability Pathway
   A linked resource described as helping educators build knowledge, confidence and cultural capability for Te ao Maori.

--- OVERALL KB NOTE ---
This resource collection is broader than assessment alone. It is useful as supporting context for culturally responsive assessment design because it emphasises Maori pedagogies, literacy and numeracy capability, and ways of engaging Maori learners through culturally grounded approaches.
