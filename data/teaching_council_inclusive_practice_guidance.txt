Inclusive Practice Guidance

Created with teachers. Grounded in real practice. Supporting every akonga.

--- WHAT IS INCLUSIVE TEACHING PRACTICE GUIDANCE (ITPG)? ---
The Inclusive Teaching Practice Guidance (ITPG) is a draft resource designed to help teachers and education leaders create learning environments where every akonga feels welcome, supported, and able to thrive.

Whether in early learning, primary, secondary, or initial teacher education, the ITPG offers practical ideas that can be used and adapted to suit learners and settings.

It is not a rulebook. It is a flexible guide with strategies, examples, and tools that can be used in ways that fit the community and context.

This resource was co-created with voices from across the education sector, including teachers, leaders, disabled people, Maori educators, inclusive education specialists, ITE providers, union representatives, and Ministry of Education advisers.

The ITPG:
- Connects inclusive education with high-quality teaching
- Builds on professional strengths and aspirations
- Embeds the values of Whakamana, Manaakitanga, Pono, and Whanaungatanga

--- WHY IT MATTERS ---
The ITPG supports teachers to meet Nga Paerewa | Standards for the Teaching Profession in ways that are practical, relevant, and grounded in inclusive values.

It builds on insights from recent ERO reports:
- Thriving at School? - Education for Disabled Learners in Schools
- A Great Start? - Education for Disabled Children in Early Childhood

The page says these reports highlighted strong inclusive practices, especially in:
- Schools with high equity index ratings, where disabled learners and whanau felt more supported
- English-medium schools with high numbers of akonga Maori, where inclusive cultures were deeply embedded

It also responds to ERO recommendations, including:
- Strengthening the focus on teaching disabled learners in ITE, induction, and mentoring
- Making inclusive practice more visible in professional standards
- Prioritising disability in professional learning and development
- Ensuring accessible, high-quality resources and support are available

--- WHAT YOU'LL FIND IN THE ITPG ---
- Real-life case studies and examples
- Strategies, tools, and ideas that can be used straight away
- Links to professional learning
- Opportunities to connect with communities of practice

The page says the resource is built on the Martyn Rouse Knowing, Believing, Doing model and guided by the values of Open Heart, Open Mind, and Open Arms, encouraging empathy, reflection, and meaningful action.

--- CURRENTLY IN TRIAL ---
The ITPG is being trialled in early learning services, schools, ITE programmes, and RTLB settings. Feedback will help shape the final version.

--- VALUES PANELS ON THE PAGE ---
Open Heart
Ahakoa iti, he iti mapihi pounamu.
The Maori perception of the tamaiti is encapsulated in this whakatauki. It refers to the singular beauty and immense value of even the tiniest piece of fine greenstone.

Open Mind
Kei tena, kei tena, kei tena ano. Tona ake ahua, tona ake mauri, tona ake mana.
Each and every one has their own uniqueness, life essence and presence.

Open Arms
Ma te huruhuru ka rere te manu.
Adorn the bird with feathers so that it may soar.
